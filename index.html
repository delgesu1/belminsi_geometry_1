Biography

Ivan Belminsi has firmly established himself as one of the most versatile and captivating cellists of his generation, known for performances that combine technical brilliance with profound emotional depth.

Born into a family of musicians, Ivan began his cello studies at the age of six. His exceptional talent was evident from an early age, leading to his acceptance at the prestigious Conservatoire National Supérieur de Musique in Paris, where he studied under renowned cellist Michel Strauss. He later continued his studies at the Juilliard School with the legendary Joel Krosnick.

"Music is not just about playing the notes correctly, but about revealing the soul behind them. Each piece has its own story waiting to be told."

Ivan's international career was launched after winning the First Prize at the International Paulo Cello Competition. Since then, he has performed with many of the world's leading orchestras, including the Berlin Philharmonic, London Symphony Orchestra, Boston Symphony, and the Orchestre de Paris. His performances at Carnegie Hall, Wigmore Hall, Salle Pleyel, and the Berlin Philharmonie have consistently received critical acclaim.

As a dedicated chamber musician, Ivan collaborates regularly with acclaimed artists and ensembles. His versatility extends to his repertoire, which spans from Bach to contemporary works, with a particular emphasis on expanding the cello literature through new commissions.

Ivan plays a rare Matteo Goffriller cello, made in Venice in 1705, kindly on loan from a private foundation.

Teaching Studio

Ivan Belminsi maintains a select teaching studio, working with advanced students and young professional cellists. His teaching philosophy combines rigorous technical foundation with profound musical understanding and historical context.

Drawing from his own diverse training and performing experiences, Ivan helps students develop their unique artistic voice while addressing the technical aspects essential for artistic freedom.

Teaching Philosophy

  • Technical mastery should serve musical expression, not exist for its own sake.
  • Understanding the historical and compositional context of each work is essential for meaningful interpretation.
  • Each student possesses unique qualities that should be nurtured and developed rather than forced into a predetermined mold.
  • The development of critical listening skills and self-assessment is crucial for continued growth beyond formal education.
  • Chamber music and collaborative skills are as important as solo playing abilities.
